1. HCI - Accessibility: Using Anonymity-Preserving Technologies to Encourage More Deaf People to Provide Data, Enabling the Possibility of Crowdsourced AI in ASL and Understanding the Behavior and Needs of Disabled People.

1.1 Understanding the uniqueness within the community, reasons for biases:

Understanding Deaf culture, the uniqueness of the Deaf community, and their ideology, especially how they view themselves in comparison to other ordinary people and their interactions with other groups. Through this research, 'entering' the Deaf community to understand their sensitivities, concerns, and needs. This aids in designing more reasonable solutions and researching how to ethically and legally obtain data.

1.2 How to Protect Participants' Privacy with Technical Methods before Data Collection:

After researching the previous paper, the biggest challenge in the current ASL research comes from participants' privacy concerns. They are unwilling to provide facial data. The focus of this step is to study how to use dynamic video filter technology to cover some identifying information, thus protecting participants' privacy and encouraging them to provide facial data.

1.3 After Researching Privacy-Preserving Technologies, How to Design an Online Platform to Make it Easier for More Participants to Contribute to Data Projects:

By technical means, provide methods of anonymity and de-identification to build an online ASL data sharing platform. This platform allows more participants to share data in a safer, cost-effective way, paving the way for future crowdsourced AI research.

2. Human-AI Interaction: Involvement in testing LLM to identify whether generated content meets human needs and reduce content bias (project link).

Research Understanding:

Based on past research and my own experience, biases in generative AI can have severe negative impacts on the fields of communication, social interaction, and media. However, if these biases persist, they might mislead users right from the start, especially causing detrimental effects on younger users. I am passionate about researching and analyzing how to identify, uncover, and reduce biases, as well as the errors in AI's understanding of humans, in order to maintain a balanced social media environment and protect and educate the youth to discern accurate information.

3. Technological Innovations and Implications for Learning

Advancements in VR/AR in Science Education

The research mentioned above pertains to XR technology implementation in virtual classrooms and its applications, encompassing the nuances of online education and collaborative digital learning. By combining cognitive psychology with the anthropology of learning processes, this research aims to address the challenges and impacts of implementing emerging educational technology on student experiences.
